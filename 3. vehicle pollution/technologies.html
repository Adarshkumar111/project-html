Technologies

Hybrid Vehicles: One of the most obvious ways to reduce vehicle emissions is to use alternative sources of power, such as batteries or fuel cells, instead of fossil fuels. Hybrid and electric vehicles (HEVs and EVs) can either combine an internal combustion engine (ICE) with an electric motor, or rely solely on electricity.

Advanced Combustion Engine: Advanced combustion engines use technologies such as direct injection, variable valve timing, turbocharging, and cylinder deactivation to optimize the fuel-air mixture, the engine speed, the air pressure, and the number of cylinders in operation. These technologies can reduce emissions by lowering the fuel consumption, increasing the power output, and minimizing the engine losses.

Alternative Fuel and Biofuel: Alternative fuels and biofuels can be derived from sources such as natural gas, hydrogen, ethanol, biodiesel, or biomass. They can reduce emissions by displacing the fossil fuels, reducing the greenhouse effect.
